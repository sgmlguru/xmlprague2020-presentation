Programming Languages as Religion

  • [G] Did an audit
  • inhouse built transformer
  • situation: small required fix => log dev time + another issue
  • Did a transform approach audit, concluding with workshop
  • everyone seemed happy, discussed migration, training
  • overheard: XSLT over my dead body => it had become religious
  • managers would ahndle that => still cancelled

We've Always Done This

Sometimes SGML Is What You Want

  • 20 years ago: aerospace client with S1000D SGML, needed new system
  • Analysis => SGML was fine, partners used SGML, publ was to SGML, etc
  • Client was fine with SGML, too
  • BUT consultancy about to merge with another, wanted new thing XML, new tools, politics, etc

=> expensive lawsuit, client bought a competitor's system

Things We Found among the Ashes

So, did we LEARN anything?

  • Be PRAGMATIC
  • Swallow your pride
  • MUK talks, Debbie and Liam
  • Engage socially (coffee, beer better than guidelines)
  • Never engage in religious discussions
  • Choose your battles
  • Do engage in licensing
  • Open source vs licensed software
  • contract negotiations
  • Don't bargain on your pricing (value, not hours)
  • Get your responsibilities straight from the start
  • Organise - make sure you can get out quickly
  • Document - you may have to come back some day
  • Accept failure gracefully
  • We're very proud people - avoid assigning blame
